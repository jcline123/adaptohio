Positive representation of limb differences helps children feel seen and confident. Here are some characters and figures we love:

Nemo (Finding Nemo)

Nemo has a smaller “lucky fin,” a subtle but powerful example of limb difference in a beloved Pixar film. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Hiccup (How to Train Your Dragon)

After losing his leg, Hiccup uses a prosthetic and becomes a confident dragon rider—showing courage and perseverance. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Massimo Marcovaldo (Luca)

Giulia’s father in Pixar’s *Luca*, Massimo was born with one arm. His appearance is brief but meaningful, showing strength and normalcy. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Ginkgo (Tumble Leaf)

This character has a wooden claw. His gentle stories teach kids about belonging and adapting. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Luna (An Almost Christmas Story)

This 2024 Disney+ short features a young girl with a limb difference. It highlights empathy and inclusion in a holiday tale. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

Star Wars Characters (Star Wars)

Iconic heroes like Luke Skywalker and Anakin, and villain Darth Maul, feature prosthetics—making limb difference part of a grand heroic narrative.

Della Duck (DuckTales 2017)

Della uses a prosthetic and is known for her strength, adventuring spirit, and bravery.
