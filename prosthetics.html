Set 1: First Prosthetics

Gwen received her first set of prosthetics around her first birthday. These featured a basic SACH foot attached directly to the socket to keep her low to the ground and stable. Her suspension system used a pe-lite liner and a Flexi Kids sleeve that rolled up to her thigh. This setup worked well for early walking and balance.

Set 2: Scout Foot & Increased Mobility

Gwen's second set of legs retained the same socket and suspension system, but introduced the Scout foot by College Park. This made a huge improvement in mobility. She got faster and more confident. The only downside was that the thigh-high sleeves became cumbersome during potty training.

Set 3: Gel Liners & Pin Locks

For the third set, we switched to gel liners with a pin lock system which stopped just above the knee—much better for potty training. Getting a good fit was tricky since pediatric gel liners are limited. We used an Iceross distal cup on one leg and Ottobock gel spots on the other.

We also tried the Truper foot, but in our experience the Scout foot provided a smoother gait. While the Truper foot offered more flexibility for uneven surfaces, Gwen seemed more stable overall in the Scout foot.

Set 4: Formula Foot

For Gwen’s fourth set, we kept the gel liner and pin lock system but switched to the Formula pediatric foot by Fillauer. This has been a game-changer. Gwen’s gait is smoother, her steps are quieter and more stable, and her confidence on stairs and inclines improved noticeably. It’s our favorite foot so far.

Skin & Prosthetic Care

We apply Aquaphor daily to keep Gwen’s skin healthy. To clean her liners and sockets, we use Aquaphor Baby Unscented Soap. We had issues with irritation from scented soap, but switching to unscented resolved that. Every few days, we also wipe down her liners with an alcohol wipe for extra cleanliness.
